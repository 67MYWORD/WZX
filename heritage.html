🏯 Echoes of 13 Dynasties

Walk where emperors walked. Xi'an wears its 3,100 years of history not in museums alone — but in the very stones beneath your feet.

The Eternal Army

Discovered by farmers digging a well in 1974, the Terracotta Army guards the tomb of Qin Shi Huang — China's first emperor. Over 8,000 life-sized soldiers, each with unique facial features, stand in battle formation. No photographs can prepare you for the sheer scale.

⭐ UNESCO World Heritage

City Wall

Ming Dynasty · 1370 AD

The most complete ancient city wall in China. Stretching 13.7 km, it's wide enough to cycle on top — rent a bike at the South Gate and ride the full loop at sunset.

💡 Fun fact: The wall is thicker at the base (15m) than it is tall (12m) — built to withstand cannon fire.

Big Wild Goose Pagoda

Tang Dynasty · 652 AD

Built to house Buddhist scriptures brought from India by the monk Xuanzang (the real-life inspiration for "Journey to the West"). Climb to the top for panoramic views of modern Xi'an.

💡 The pagoda has survived over a dozen major earthquakes — it tilts slightly but remains standing.

Bell Tower

Ming Dynasty · 1384 AD

The geographical center of ancient Chang'an. This wooden tower marks the intersection of Xi'an's four main avenues. Visit at dusk when the surrounding lights create a magical glow.

💡 The massive bronze bell inside was used to mark time — its sound could be heard across the entire walled city.

Drum Tower

Ming Dynasty · 1380 AD

Sister to the Bell Tower, the Drum Tower signaled the end of the day. Inside, you'll find an impressive collection of drums from across China's history. The view over the Muslim Quarter is unmatched.

💡 There's a drum performance every afternoon — the rhythm echoes ancient timekeeping traditions.

Small Wild Goose Pagoda

Tang Dynasty · 707 AD

Less crowded than its bigger sibling, this elegant brick pagoda sits within the serene Jianfu Temple grounds. The adjacent Xi'an Museum offers excellent context without the crowds.

💡 The top two levels were sheared off by an earthquake in 1556 — and were never rebuilt.

Huaqing Palace

Tang Dynasty · Hot Springs

Imperial hot spring resort at the foot of Mount Li. This is where Emperor Xuanzong and his beloved concubine Yang Guifei spent winters. The setting is especially romantic in spring when cherry blossoms bloom.

💡 The natural hot springs still flow at 43°C — you can dip your hands in the public pools.
